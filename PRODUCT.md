# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small and medium business owners (plus startup founders and professionals needing portfolios) evaluating Gencolink as a software development partner. They arrive from referrals or search, usually on a first visit, deciding whether this team is competent and trustworthy enough to contact. The job to be done: "convince me these people can build my software reliably."

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Gencolink, a software development company (custom software, business management systems, web apps, portfolios, automation). The single conversion goal is the contact form — every section builds enough trust to justify sending a message. Content (services, values, FAQs, section copy, company details) is CMS-driven from Directus so the team can update it without redeploys.

## Brand Personality

Professional, precise, engineered. Confidence through craft, not hype. The interface should feel like it was built by the same careful engineers the visitor is about to hire — the site itself is the first proof of quality. Emotional goals: trust, competence, calm assurance.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing template: identical icon-card grids, gradient text, hero-metric rows.
- Anything that looks mass-produced or template-bought.
- Loud startup hype (rocket emojis, "10x", exclamation marks).

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the portfolio.** Every detail (loading states, motion, typography) is evidence of engineering quality.
2. **Calm precision.** Restrained navy/blue palette, generous whitespace, technical mono accents — nothing shouting.
3. **Content-first honesty.** CMS data is the real content; never present stale fallback copy as if it were live.
4. **Motion with intent.** Reveal animations support reading order; nothing bounces or loops for decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG mandate; hold to WCAG AA as baseline (contrast ≥4.5:1 body text, visible focus, reduced-motion alternatives for every animation, semantic landmarks). Site already uses aria-labels and semantic sections — keep that bar.
